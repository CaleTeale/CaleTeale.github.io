Photography
Since my first family holiday I have always had an interest in photography and certain techniques. Despite many years of taking photos, there is still much for me to learn, however, even through small sessions experimenting and testing different styles and subjects - I know I have an immense passion for the field of photography. My favourite technique (so far) is low light photography. Capturing the desired amount of light requires extreme patience, but the results are highly worthwhile and can exhibit many hidden meanings and stories.

Photography comes hand in hand with both of my other passions - Video Editing and Graphic Design. Having all three of these fields come together to create art and entertainment is amazing to be a part of, and being able to do it all personally is even greater a feeling.

I edit my photos with methods such as: colour grading and artifical focus fields which further emphasize the subject and environment. Bringing them to a further level of quality. I never veer too far from the original photo, the changes simply make the photo 'pop' better and achieve effects that require equipment I don't have.

VIDEO EDITING

One day in Year 7, I realised how much content I watched across YouTube and decided I wanted to have a go at being a part of it. I created a channel, downloaded the trial of Adobe After Effects and spent a weekend learning and testing. I watched tutorials, created my own videos and short films, and realised how much I enjoyed the whole process of learning different techniques and creating. I never looked at a film or video the same again. Every video I tried to do something new. I remember the first special effect I ever did was cloning myself in After Effects using the mask tool. In the absence of other actors to interact with I would substitute myself into the scene and time everything so it would appear I was holding a conversation with myself. One time I even managed to throw something to myself!

Once the trial ran out I bought the latest Adobe Production Premium Creative suite. Containing Photoshop, After Effects, Premiere Pro, Illustrator and Flash. Without this realisation back in Year 7 I never would have discovered any of these passions I have today. I am highly confident in my editing abilities today, but there is always much more to learn.

Creating these YouTube videos lead to the introduction of the use of Photoshop and Illustrator in the whole production process. From creating 'muzzle flashes' with transparent background to make my fake guns seem real, to creating thumbnails for the videos I uploaded. This thumbnail creation introduced me to the whole Graphic Design field and what I could create with knowledge in it.
Whenever I am asked which I prefer it is always a hard decision between Video Editing and Graphic Design. Am I allowed to say I love both equally? I am completely self-taught in this field. All the artwork above was created without any tutorials or guidance. I simply sat down one day, got to know what was what and was determined to know more to be able to create all those posters and graphics seen everywhere.

I stepped forward one time to design the logo for my school's Film Club, and since then I was the 'go-to' for graphic design work in my school. I created event posters, club logos, designs for my assignments. Every opportunity I had to create a graphic I created one. At the year of my graduation I got a special mention in the school yearbook for being the "resident graphic designer for the school" from the lead teacher in IT. I am still discovering new things I can do in Photoshop and Illustrator and will always be continuing to create into the future, no matter the career I have.
